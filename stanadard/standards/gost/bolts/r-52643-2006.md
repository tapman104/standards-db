# Standard Overview

GOST R 52643-2006 is a modern Russian national standard (the "R" prefix designates a national standard of the Russian Federation, distinct from the inherited Soviet GOST system). It establishes general specifications for high-strength bolt assemblies used in metal load-bearing structures. It covers the complete bolting system: mechanical properties, surface treatment, marking, packaging, and acceptance testing for bolts, nuts, and washers used together as a pretensioned assembly.

This standard aligns with European EN 14399 series philosophy while retaining Russian-specific material grade designations. It supersedes the older GOST 22353-77 approach for modern Russian structural engineering projects designed under current codes.

Dimensional requirements are covered in GOST R 52644-2006 (companion standard). GOST R 52643-2006 focuses on system-level requirements applicable to the full bolt assembly.

# Materials

Alloy steels per Russian GOST material standards. Closely controlled chemistry for consistent pretension behaviour in structural assemblies. Specific grades per GOST R 52643-2006 Table 1.

# Surface Treatments

- Hot-dip galvanizing (most common for outdoor structures)
- Bare (indoor protected structures)
- Zinc electroplating with mandatory de-embrittlement bake for class 10.9

# Marking Requirements

- Property class "10.9" on head
- Manufacturer code
- GOST R 52643 identification
- Assembly lot traceability required
