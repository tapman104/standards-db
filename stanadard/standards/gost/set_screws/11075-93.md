# Standard Overview

GOST 11075-93 specifies set screws with a dog (pin) point — a short cylindrical protrusion that engages a corresponding machined depression or hole in the mating shaft. This design provides both axial and rotational locking, making it the strongest holding set screw configuration. The pin can be full-length or half-length depending on the combination of screw diameter and length.

Direct equivalents are ISO 4028 and the withdrawn DIN 915. Applications include gear-to-shaft connections, pulley hubs, cam assemblies, and any precision location where the screw must both lock and locate a component on a shaft.

**Full dog vs. half dog rule (ISO 4028):**
- M8, length ≤ 10 mm → half dog (pin length z = 2–2.25 mm)
- M8, length > 10 mm → full dog (pin length z = 4–4.3 mm)
- Verify for each size/length combination in the standard table

**Point behaviour:** Dog point engages a machined hole or slot in the mating shaft, providing positive axial AND rotational locking. Highest holding power of all set screw types. No surface damage to shaft — pin enters pre-machined recess without scoring.

# Installation Requirement

A machined hole or recess must be pre-drilled in the shaft to accept the dog point pin. Without this recess:
- The pin will ride on the shaft surface and shear under torque
- Rotational locking will not be achieved
- Surface of shaft will be damaged

Recess depth: approximately 0.5–0.8 × pin length for full engagement. Recess diameter: H7 fit on the dog pin diameter.

# Reusability

Dog point set screws are fully reusable. The pin engages a machined recess without causing further deformation. Inspect the pin for wear and the socket for rounding. Replace if pin diameter is visibly worn.
