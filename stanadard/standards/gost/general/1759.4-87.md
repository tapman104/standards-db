# Standard Overview

GOST 1759.4-87 specifies the mechanical and physical property requirements for metric fasteners (bolts, screws, and studs) across all property classes from 3.6 to 12.9. It defines minimum tensile strength, proof load stress, yield point, elongation, impact energy, and hardness for each property class.

The property class marking system — where the first digit × 100 = nominal tensile strength in MPa and the product of both digits × 10 = nominal yield strength — is consistent with ISO 898-1. The Indian equivalent IS 1367 Part 3 adopts the same system. This standard is the mechanical backbone of the entire GOST fastener series.

# Property Class System

The property class designation (e.g., 8.8, 10.9, 12.9) encodes the mechanical properties:
- **First digit × 100** = nominal ultimate tensile strength (MPa)
- **Both digits product × 10** = nominal lower yield stress or 0.2% proof stress (MPa)

Example: Property class 10.9 → 10 × 100 = 1000 MPa tensile; 10 × 9 × 10 = 900 MPa yield

# Materials per Property Class

| Property Class | Steel Category | Typical GOST Grade | Heat Treatment |
|----------------|---------------|--------------------|-|
| 4.6 | Carbon steel | Steel 10, Steel 20 | None (as-drawn) |
| 5.6/5.8 | Carbon steel | Steel 20, Steel 35 | As-drawn or annealed |
| 8.8 (≤M16) | Carbon or carbon-boron | Steel 35 | Q&T |
| 8.8 (>M16) | Cr-alloy | Steel 40X | Q&T |
| 10.9 | Cr-Mn-Si alloy | Steel 30HGSA, 40X | Q&T |
| 12.9 | Ni-Mo or Cr-Mn-Si alloy | 35HGSA, 40HN2MA | Q&T, controlled chemistry |

**Class 12.9 material restrictions:** S ≤ 0.035%, P ≤ 0.035%. No conventional acid-bath electroplating.
