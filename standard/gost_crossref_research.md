## SECTION 1: CORE HEX BOLTS

---

### GOST 7798-70
**Title:** Hexagon Head Bolt, Product Grade B (Partial Thread)  
**Category:** Core bolt  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| ISO 4014 | ISO | Direct equivalent — partial thread, grades A/B |
| DIN 931 | DIN | Dimensionally near-identical; minor WAF diff at M10/M12/M14/M22 |
| IS 1364-1 | BIS | Indian equivalent for hex head bolts with shank |
| ASTM A307 | ASTM | Approximate; lower grade carbon steel bolts |

**Dimension facts (public domain):**
- Thread range: M6 to M48
- Maximum length: 300 mm
- Thread tolerance: 6g
- Product grade: B (accuracy class B in GOST terminology)

**Property classes covered:** 4.6, 5.6, 5.8, 8.8, 10.9

**Original Summary:**
GOST 7798-70 specifies hexagon head bolts with a partially threaded shank, produced to
accuracy class B. The bolt features a hexagonal head and a cylindrical shank with thread
covering only part of the bolt length, making it suitable for joints where the bolt passes
through clearance holes and clamps two or more parts together. Thread diameters range from
M6 to M48, with lengths up to 300 mm. It is widely used in mechanical engineering,
structural steel fabrication, pipeline flanges, and equipment installation. The standard
corresponds closely to ISO 4014 and the German DIN 931, with minor dimensional differences
at specific sizes (M10, M12, M14, M22) in the width across flats. Indian equivalent is
IS 1364 Part 1.

**Data sources consulted:** kalibrmetiz.com, iso.org, wermac.org, fasteners.eu

### GOST 7805-70
**Title:** Hexagon Head Bolt, Full Thread (Thread to Head)  
**Category:** Core bolt  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| ISO 4017 | ISO | Direct equivalent — full thread hex bolt |
| DIN 933 | DIN | Direct equivalent; same dimensions as ISO 4017 except minor WAF at M10/M12/M14/M22 |
| IS 1364-2 | BIS | Indian equivalent for hex head screws (full thread) |
| ASTM A307 | ASTM | Approximate |

**Dimension facts:**
- Thread range: M1.6 to M64 (ISO 4017 scope)
- Thread tolerance: 6g
- Product grades: A (M1.6–M24, length ≤ 10d or 150mm) and B (M24+)
- Full thread runs entire shank length

**Property classes covered:** 4.6, 5.6, 5.8, 8.8, 10.9, 12.9; Stainless A2, A4

**Original Summary:**
GOST 7805-70 defines fully threaded hexagon head bolts where the metric thread extends the
entire length of the shank from the head. This design is preferred when bolts thread directly
into tapped holes or when maximum thread engagement is required. Available across a wide
diameter range with property classes from 4.6 up to 12.9, and in stainless steel grades A2
and A4. The standard aligns directly with ISO 4017 and its German counterpart DIN 933. The
Indian equivalent is IS 1364 Part 2. Key application areas include mechanical assemblies,
agricultural equipment, construction fixings, and food-industry equipment when manufactured
in stainless steel.

**Data sources consulted:** dinmark.com.ua, iso.org, bdfastener.com, IS 1364 documentation

**Title:** Hexagon Head Bolt, Product Grade C  
**Category:** Core bolt

## SECTION 2: HIGH-STRENGTH / STRUCTURAL BOLTS

---

### GOST 22353-77
**Title:** High-Strength Hexagon Head Bolt for Steel Structures  
**Category:** Structural / high-strength  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| ISO 7411 | ISO | High-strength structural bolt, product grade C, classes 8.8/10.9 |
| DIN 6914 / EN 14399-4 | DIN/EN | Direct German/European equivalent |
| IS 3757 | BIS | Indian high-strength structural bolt standard |
| ASTM A325 | ASTM | Approximate US equivalent |

**Dimension facts:**
- Thread range: M12 to M36
- Property class: 10.9 minimum
- Material: alloy steel
- Large width across flats (structural head)

**Original Summary:**
GOST 22353-77 specifies high-strength hexagon head bolts designed exclusively for
structural steel connections subject to heavy static and dynamic loading, including
vibration-prone environments. Manufactured from alloy steel at a minimum property class
of 10.9, these bolts have enlarged head dimensions to provide greater bearing surface
against structural members. They are installed with matched high-strength nuts (GOST 22354)
and hardened washers (GOST 22355) as a complete bolting assembly. The standard aligns with
ISO 7411 and the European EN 14399-4 (formerly DIN 6914). The Indian equivalent is IS 3757,
which is specified for structural steel connections in bridges, industrial frames, and
high-rise buildings.

**Data sources consulted:** dinmark.com.ua, IS 3757 PDF, iso.org/standard/14140

### GOST 5915-70
**Title:** Hexagon Nut, Style 1, Product Grade B  
**Category:** Nut  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| ISO 4032 | ISO | Hexagon regular nut, style 1, grades A and B |
| DIN 934 | DIN | Direct equivalent (now superseded by ISO 4032 in DIN system) |
| IS 1364-3 | BIS | Identical to ISO 4032 per BIS documentation |

**Dimension facts:**
- Thread range: M1.6 to M64
- Style 1 nut height: ~0.8d
- Property classes: 5, 6, 8, 10, 12
- Thread tolerance: 6H

**Original Summary:**
GOST 5915-70 defines the standard hexagon nut (style 1) with normal height, produced to
accuracy class B. It is the most commonly paired nut for use with GOST 7798-70 and
GOST 7805-70 bolts in general mechanical and structural applications. Style 1 nuts have a
height approximately 0.8 times the nominal thread diameter. The standard is dimensionally
equivalent to ISO 4032 and the now-withdrawn DIN 934, both of which share the same
width-across-flats and height dimensions. The Indian standard IS 1364 Part 3 is declared
identical to ISO 4032 by BIS. Property classes from 5 through 12 are available.

## SECTION 5: WASHERS

---

### GOST 11371-78
**Title:** Plain (Flat) Washer  
**Category:** Washer  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| ISO 7089 | ISO | Plain washer without chamfer (replaces DIN 125A) |
| ISO 7090 | ISO | Plain washer with chamfer (replaces DIN 125B) |
| DIN 125 | DIN | Legacy equivalent; DIN 125A=ISO 7089, DIN 125B=ISO 7090 |
| IS 2016 | BIS | Indian plain washer standard |

**Dimension facts:**
- Thread range: M1 to M48
- Two versions: Version 1 (no chamfer = ISO 7089), Version 2 (chamfer on outer edge = ISO 7090)
- Two accuracy classes: A (precision) and C (general)
- Materials: steel, stainless steel, aluminium alloys (AD1, AMg)
- Hardness: 200 HV (standard), 300 HV (for use with class 10.9 bolts)

**Original Summary:**
GOST 11371-78 specifies flat circular washers used to distribute clamping load under bolt
heads or nuts, protecting the mating surface and bridging oversized holes. Two versions are
defined: Version 1 without outer chamfer (equivalent to ISO 7089 / DIN 125A) and Version 2
with chamfer on the outer edge (equivalent to ISO 7090 / DIN 125B). Accuracy class A offers
tighter dimensional control for precision applications; class C is suitable for general
construction. The standard covers thread diameters M1 through M48, available in carbon
steel, stainless steel, and aluminium alloys for specialised pneumatic and hydraulic
applications. Hardness selection (200 HV or 300 HV) should match the bolt property class
per ISO guidance.

**Data sources consulted:** ukrstarline.ua, eurolinkfss.com, brasscomponents.fr, aspenfasteners.com

### GOST 6402-70
**Title:** Spring (Lock) Washer — Grover Washer  
**Category:** Washer  

**Cross-References:**
| Standard | Body | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| DIN 127 Form B | DIN | Direct equivalent for GOST 6402-70 Version 1 |
| DIN 127 Form A | DIN | Equivalent for GOST 6402-70 Version 2 |
| ISO 7090 | ISO | Note: ISO 7090 is a plain chamfered washer — NOT the spring washer |
| IS 3063 | BIS | Indian spring washer standard |

**⚠ IMPORTANT NOTE:** DIN 127 covers M2–M100; GOST 6402-70 covers M2–M48 only.
Minor differences in outer diameter and thickness between DIN 127 and GOST 6402-70 exist.

**Dimension facts:**
- Thread range: M2 to M48
- Four types: H (normal, square section), T (heavy), OT (extra heavy), L (light, rectangular section)
- Material: spring steel 65G (standard), steel 70 (higher hardness variant), stainless A2/A4
- Hardness: 41.5–49.5 HRC (steel); ≥ 90 HRB (bronze)
- Designed for bolts/studs of property class ≤ 5.8

**Original Summary:**
GOST 6402-70 specifies single-coil split spring washers (known colloquially as Grover
washers) used to prevent spontaneous loosening of threaded connections under vibration and
dynamic loads. The washer functions by the split ends biting into the nut and bearing
surface when reverse rotation is attempted. Four cross-section types are defined ranging
from light to extra-heavy duty. The standard covers M2 to M48 and is intended for use only
with bolts of property class 5.8 or lower — using spring washers with class 8.8 or higher
bolts is not recommended as the hardened bolt may override the spring effect. The direct
German equivalent is DIN 127 (Form B for Version 1, Form A for Version 2). The Indian
equivalent is IS 3063.

**Data sources consulted:** apsrus.ru, dinmark.com.ua, metalfastenersind.com, fasenco.com

## SECTION 7: THREAD STANDARDS

### GOST 9150-2002
**Title:** Metric Thread — Basic Profile  
**Cross-References:** ISO 68-1, IS 4218 Part 1  

### GOST 8724-2002
**Title:** Metric Thread — Diameters and Pitches  
**Cross-References:** ISO 261, ISO 262, IS 4218 Part 2  

### GOST 24705-2004
**Title:** Metric Thread — Basic Dimensions  
**Cross-References:** ISO 724, IS 4218 Part 3  

**Unified Summary (thread standards):**
The three GOST thread standards collectively define the Russian metric thread system.
GOST 9150-2002 establishes the fundamental 60-degree thread profile, identical to ISO 68-1.
GOST 8724-2002 specifies the preferred diameter-pitch combinations for coarse and fine
metric threads, matching ISO 261/262. GOST 24705-2004 provides the calculated basic
dimensions (pitch diameter, minor diameter, etc.) consistent with ISO 724. Together these
three standards are fully harmonised with the international ISO metric thread system and
the Indian IS 4218 series, meaning any metric fastener manufactured to GOST thread
specifications is dimensionally interchangeable with ISO/IS/DIN metric threaded fasteners.

## SECTION 8: MARKING REQUIREMENTS

**Governing standards:** ISO 898-1 Clause 11, GOST 1759.1-82, IS 1367-13

### Bolt and Screw Head Marking

Property class is marked on the top bearing face or the hexagon side face of the head.
Manufacturer identification code is required alongside property class for class 8.8 and above.

| Property Class | Tensile (MPa) | Marking mandatory from | Location |
|----------------|---------------|------------------------|----------|
| 4.6 | 400 | M5 and above | Head face or side |
| 5.6 | 500 | M5 and above | Head face or side |
| 5.8 | 500 | M5 and above | Head face or side |
| 8.8 | 800 | All diameters | Head face or side |
| 10.9 | 1000 | All diameters | Head face or side |
| 12.9 | 1200 | All diameters | Head side only — embossed, never indented |

**⚠ CLASS 12.9 MARKING RULE:** Property class marks on class 12.9 fasteners must be embossed (raised) or applied to the side face — never stamped as indentations on the bearing face. Indentation creates stress concentration and can initiate fracture in the high-strength material.

Stainless steel fasteners are marked with combined grade + strength code: **A2-70**, **A4-70**, **A4-80**, etc. The letter-number prefix identifies the steel alloy family; the two-digit suffix is the minimum tensile strength in units of 10 MPa.

### Nut Marking

Marked on the side face of one hexagon flat. For nuts smaller than M5, marking may be omitted.

| Property Class | Mark on flat | Notes |
|----------------|-------------|-------|
| 5 | "5" | Carbon steel |
| 6 | "6" | Carbon steel |
| 8 | "8" | Alloy or carbon steel |
| 10 | "10" | Alloy steel |
| 12 | "12" | Alloy steel |
| A2 stainless | "A2" | Combined with strength code if applicable |
| A4 stainless | "A4" | Combined with strength code if applicable |

### GOST-Specific Marking (GOST 1759.1-82)

Russian-manufactured fasteners carry three marking elements on the head:
1. **Property class** (e.g., 8.8, 10.9)
2. **Manufacturer code** — abbreviated plant designation registered with Rosstandart
3. **Thread direction** — left-hand thread marked "LH"; right-hand thread unmarked by convention
4. **Coating code** — not marked on the fastener itself; stated in the ordering designation string

Fine-pitch thread designation: always written explicitly in the designation string as M12×1.25. Coarse pitch (standard) threads do not require pitch notation in the marking.

## SECTION 9: SURFACE TREATMENTS & COATINGS

**Governing standards:** ISO 4042 (electroplating), ISO 10684 (hot-dip galvanizing),
GOST 9.301-86 (metallic coatings — general requirements), GOST 9.307-89 (hot-dip zinc)

### Coating Code Table (GOST 9.301 designation system)

| GOST Code | Treatment | Equivalent | Min Thickness | Corrosion resistance (salt spray) |
|-----------|-----------|------------|---------------|----------------------------------|
| 00 | Bare / black (no coating) | — | — | Protection from oil/grease only |
| 01 | Zinc electroplating, yellow chromate | ISO 4042 Zn.6 ye | 6 µm | ~96 hours |
| 03 | Zinc electroplating, clear chromate | ISO 4042 Zn.9 cl | 9 µm | ~144 hours |
| 12 | Thick zinc electroplating | ISO 4042 Zn.12 | 12 µm | ~200 hours |
| 13 | Hot-dip galvanizing (HDG) | ISO 10684 | 45–85 µm | 1000+ hours |
| 40 | Phosphate + oil (Parkerizing) | GOST 9.301 Fos/м | ~5 µm | ~24 hours (indoor) |
| Ст | Stainless steel — no coating | — | — | Depends on grade |
| — | Geomet / Dacromet (zinc flake) | ISO 10683 | 8–12 µm | 480–1000 hours |

**⚠ HYDROGEN EMBRITTLEMENT WARNING — CLASS 10.9 AND 12.9:**
Acid-bath electroplating (zinc, nickel, cadmium) introduces atomic hydrogen into the steel lattice. In high-strength fasteners (class 10.9, 12.9), this causes delayed brittle fracture under sustained load — often hours or days after assembly with no visible warning.

Mandatory countermeasures per ISO 4042 Clause 8 and GOST 9.301:
- **De-embrittlement bake:** 190–210°C for minimum 4 hours within 4 hours of plating completion
- **Alternative:** Use mechanical zinc plating (no acid exposure), Geomet, or Dacromet for class 10.9/12.9 — these processes do not introduce hydrogen
- **Cadmium plating:** Restricted under RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU and REACH; use only where specifically required (aerospace)

### Hot-Dip Galvanizing (GOST 9.307-89 / ISO 10684)

Applied at approximately 450°C zinc bath temperature. Coating thickness:
- M6–M10: minimum 45 µm
- M12–M36: minimum 55 µm
- Above M36: minimum 70 µm

**HDG and thread tolerances:** Zinc coating adds 50–80 µm per surface on threads. Bolts intended for HDG are manufactured with a 6az thread tolerance (slightly undersized) to maintain 6g post-coating. HDG nuts must be tapped oversize after coating to accommodate the zinc on the bolt thread. Matched bolt-and-nut sets from HDG must always be ordered and installed together.

### Stainless Steel — Passivation

Austenitic stainless (A2, A4) develops a self-repairing chromium oxide passive film in air and most oxidising environments. No additional coating is required or recommended. Mechanical damage (scratching) to this layer will self-heal in the presence of oxygen. In chloride-rich environments (marine, chemical), A4 (316 with molybdenum) is required; A2 (304) is susceptible to pitting corrosion.

## SECTION 13: INSTALLATION TORQUE REFERENCE

**Basis:** Indicative tightening torques calculated for 90% of proof load utilisation,
friction coefficient µ = 0.14 (standard for zinc-plated, clean, dry threads).

**Adjustments for actual conditions:**
- Lubricated with oil or wax: multiply by **0.75**
- PTFE / Molykote G-Rapid (anti-seize): multiply by **0.65**
- Galvanized (HDG) threads, unlubricated: multiply by **1.15** (higher friction)
- Stainless A2-70 / A4-70, no anti-seize: multiply by **0.75** relative to class 8.8

**⚠ CAUTION:** These values are for reference only. Structural connections, pressure vessel
joints, and safety-critical applications require torque values determined by a qualified
engineer based on clamping force calculations for the specific joint.

### High-Strength Structural Bolts (GOST 22353-77 / IS 3757)

Structural bolts are tightened by either:
1. **Calibrated torque method** (torque wrench to specified value above)
2. **Turn-of-nut method:** Snug tight + defined rotation angle (typically 120°–240° depending on grip length)
3. **Direct tension indicator (DTI) washers:** Protrusions collapse at specified clamping force

Per GOST 22353 / IS 3757, class 10.9 structural bolts M20: approximately 390 Nm when tightened to full pretension. Refer to project specification.

### Foundation Bolts (GOST 24379 series)

Foundation bolt torque is determined by the equipment anchor plan — typically 70–80% of proof load. Exact values are outside the scope of this document and must be defined in the equipment installation manual.

## SECTION 14: REUSABILITY GUIDELINES

### Standard Hex Bolts and Nuts (GOST 7798/7805/5915/5927 series)

General hex bolts and nuts can be reused provided all of the following conditions are met:
- The bolt was tightened below its yield point (no torque-to-yield or angle-to-yield method used)
- Threads show no galling, cross-threading, deformation, or corrosion pitting when inspected
- Plating or coating remains functionally intact (minor cosmetic marks acceptable)
- For class 10.9 and 12.9: replace after any suspected overload, impact, or unknown installation history

Maximum reuse cycles recommended:
| Property class | Max reuse cycles (unlubricated) | Max reuse cycles (lubricated) |
|----------------|--------------------------------|------------------------------|
| 4.6, 5.8 | 10+ | 10+ |
| 8.8 | 5 | 10 |
| 10.9 | 3 | 5 |
| 12.9 | 2 | 3 |

### High-Strength Structural Bolts (GOST 22353-77 / EN 14399-4)

**Single use only** in friction-grip (slip-critical) structural connections. The standard (EN 14399-1 Clause 8 and IS 3757 equivalent) explicitly prohibits reuse of bolts that have been fully pretensioned. Reason: the bolt may have experienced micro-yielding at the thread roots and head-to-shank transition; retightening to the same pretension is unreliable.

### Lock Nuts — Nylon Insert (ISO 7040 / DIN 985)

| Cycle | Condition for acceptance |
|-------|--------------------------|
| 1st assembly | Full nylon engagement; prevailing torque meets ISO 2320 minimum |
| 2nd–5th assembly | Prevailing torque on each "on" cycle must still meet minimum value |
| 6th assembly and beyond | Replace — insert no longer provides reliable locking torque |

**ISO 2320 minimum prevailing torques (class 6, coarse thread, 5th cycle, dry):**
| Thread | Min torque 1st cycle (Nm) | Min torque 5th cycle (Nm) |
|--------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| M6 | 1.8 | 1.2 |
| M8 | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| M10 | 5.0 | 3.4 |
| M12 | 8.0 | 5.4 |
| M16 | 15 | 10 |
| M20 | 25 | 17 |

If the measured prevailing torque on any "on" cycle falls below the above minimum, the nut must be discarded regardless of cycle count.

**Temperature exposure:** Any nylon insert nut that has been exposed to temperatures above 120°C is single-use only — heat degrades the polyamide insert and eliminates reliable prevailing torque on subsequent assemblies.

### Lock Nuts — All-Metal Prevailing Torque (ISO 7042 / DIN 980)

Maximum 3–5 reuse cycles depending on material grade and bolt diameter. Measure prevailing torque after each removal cycle. The distorted top section of the nut plastically deforms further with each use; once the section can no longer generate adequate locking torque, replace the nut. There is no visual indicator of wear — torque testing is the only reliable check.

### Spring Washers (GOST 6402-70 / DIN 127)

**Single use only.** When compressed under a nut during assembly, the split ends lose their spring-back. Reused spring washers provide negligible locking action. Discard after each assembly.

### Plain Washers (GOST 11371-78 / ISO 7089)

Reusable indefinitely provided the bearing faces show no embedment damage, distortion, or corrosion that would affect load distribution.

### Foundation Bolts (GOST 24379 series)

Cast-in foundation bolts are permanent once embedded in concrete. Removable sleeve-type bolts (Type 5/6 in GOST 24379.1): the sleeve and nut are reusable; the bolt itself should be replaced if signs of corrosion or bending are present before re-casting.
