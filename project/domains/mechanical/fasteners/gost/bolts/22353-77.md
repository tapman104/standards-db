# Standard Overview

GOST 22353-77 specifies high-strength hexagon head bolts designed exclusively for structural steel connections subject to heavy static and dynamic loading, including vibration-prone environments. Manufactured from alloy steel at a minimum property class of 10.9, these bolts have enlarged head dimensions (large width across flats) to provide greater bearing surface against structural members and to accommodate the higher installation torques required.

These bolts are always installed as a complete bolting assembly with matched high-strength nuts (GOST 22354) and hardened washers (GOST 22355). The standard aligns with ISO 7411 and the European EN 14399-4 (formerly DIN 6914). The Indian equivalent is IS 3757, specified for structural steel connections in bridges, industrial frames, and high-rise buildings. The US approximate equivalent is ASTM A325.

# Mechanical Properties

| Property Class | Min Tensile (MPa) | Min Yield (MPa) | Hardness (HV) |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| 10.9 | 1000 | 900 | 310–372 |

Minimum property class is 10.9. No lower property classes are applicable for structural bolts under this standard.

Tightening torque (approximate, for reference only — follow project specification):
- M20, class 10.9: ~390 Nm to full structural pretension
- Exact values per project structural engineer's specification

Material must meet minimum Charpy impact energy at −20°C to ensure brittle fracture resistance in structural service.

# Surface Treatments

| Treatment | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Hot-dip galvanizing (GOST 9.307 / ISO 10684) | Standard for outdoor structural applications |
| Bare / black oxide | Indoor protected steel structures |
| Mechanical zinc plating | Avoids hydrogen embrittlement for class 10.9 |

HDG structural bolts: bolt tapped oversize; nut tapped oversize after galvanizing. Bolt-nut-washer sets must be matched from the same coating batch.

# Marking Requirements

- Property class "10.9" on head face or side flat
- Manufacturer code mandatory
- GOST 22353 identification
- Assembly sets (bolt + nut + washer) are marked and shipped as matched sets

# Tightening Methods

Three approved methods for pretensioned structural connections:
1. **Calibrated torque wrench** — to specified torque value per project document
2. **Turn-of-nut method** — snug tight + defined rotation (typically 120°–240° depending on grip length)
3. **Direct tension indicator (DTI) washers** — protrusions collapse at defined clamping force

# Testing Requirements

| Test | Standard | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Tensile strength | ISO 898-1 / GOST 1759.4 | Min 1000 MPa |
| Proof load | ISO 898-1 | No permanent deformation |
| Hardness | ISO 6507 | 310–372 HV |
| Charpy impact | ISO 148-1 | 25 J minimum at −20°C |
| Torque-tension (assembly test) | EN 14399-2 / IS 3757 | Pretension verifiable on site |

**Structural bolts are single-use only.** Once pretensioned, do not remove and reuse. The bolt may have experienced micro-yielding; retightening to the same pretension is unreliable.

# Reliability Notes

- HIGH confidence: ISO 7411 / DIN 6914 / EN 14399-4 cross-reference confirmed
- IS 3757 = functional equivalent; dimensional verification recommended for critical projects
- ASTM A325 is inch-series only — not dimensionally interchangeable
- **Single-use rule:** EN 14399-1 Clause 8 and IS 3757 prohibit reuse of pretensioned structural bolts
